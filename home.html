Who are DREAMers?

The word DREAMer is in itself an acronym because the first part of the word (DREAM) stands for Development, Relief, and Education for Alien Minors. This comes from the DREAM Act which was first introduced to legislation in 2001 and was meant to provide a pathway to citizenship for people who were brought over to the United States as children and have grown up to be an American in every sense of the word except on paper.

Another legislation was introduced to during Obama's administration which was called DACA. This stood for Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals and was proposed by Obama as an Executive order meaning presidents after him can rescind the act. The DACA act still does not lead to citizenship because it only allows for DREAMers to obtain a work visa. They can obtain a work visa by officially 'coming out' filling out an application and going through a background check.
