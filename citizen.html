Citizenship Process

Statistics

An average of 700,000 people a year become naturalized citizens of the United States.

General Steps

https://www.uscis.gov/citizenship/learners/apply-citizenship
  1. Determine if you are already a U.S. citizen.
  2. Determine if you are eligible to become a U.S. citizen
  3. Prepare your Form N-400, Application for Naturalization.
  4. Submit your form
  5. Go to the biometrics appoitment, if applicable.
  6. Complete the interview
  7. Receive a decision from USCIS on your Form N-400, Application for Naturalization.

Study for the Test

https://www.uscis.gov/citizenship/learners/study-test https://www.uscis.gov/citizenship/educators/educational-products
    Speaking Test
    Need to be able to speak English but this will be determined by a USCIS Officer during your eligibility interview on Form N-400,Application for Naturalization.
    Reading Test
    Must read one sentence of three correctly to demonstarte an ability to read in English.
    Writing Test
    Must write one sentence of three correctly to demonstrate the ability to write in English.
    Civics Test
    During the interview you will be asked 10 from the list of 100 questions(on the Civics Test), you must get six out of the ten questions to pass the civics test.
    You have two opportunities to take the English and civics test per application.
https://www.uscis.gov/news/news-releases/uscis-announces-citizenship-and-assimilation-grant-opportunities

Since 2009, the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services(USCIS) department has been offering grant opportunities to organizations that help prepare U.S. citizenship applicants. If you are part of an organization or know of one that could benefit from this opportunity, check out their website.

Get Help

There are places in your community where you can get help but not many are USCIS-Funded Programs. You can find English and Citizenship Classes in these places depending on what state you live in.
